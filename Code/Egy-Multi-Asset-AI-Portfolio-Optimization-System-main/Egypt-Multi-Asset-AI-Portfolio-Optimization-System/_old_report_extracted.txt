1. Abstract 
This project develops a comprehensive data science framework to optimize investment portfolio 
allocation within Egypt’s volatile emerging market. Addressing the challenges of currency devaluation, 
high inflation, and fragmented financial data, we constructed a reproducible pipeline to process daily 
historical prices (2015–2026) across equity indices, real estate stocks, gold, and sovereign T-bills. 
Through rigorous exploratory data analysis and feature engineering, we identified distinct risk-return 
profiles: gold consistently acted as a macroeconomic hedge with minimal drawdowns, T-bills provided a 
stable risk-free anchor, while equities exhibited high volatility and fat-tailed return distributions. Machine 
learning models—including Logistic Regression, XGBoost, Random Forest, and SVR—were deployed to 
classify market regimes and predict daily returns, achieving robust performance by leveraging technical 
indicators and volatility-aware segmentation. Monte Carlo optimization and backtesting further validated 
a diversified portfolio strategy that historically outperformed traditional benchmarks like the EGX30. We 
conclude that a dynamic, multi-asset approach balancing growth equities, real estate exposure, gold 
hedging, and T-bill stabilization significantly enhances risk-adjusted returns. Investors should prioritize 
uncorrelated asset integration and volatility-triggered rebalancing to navigate Egypt’s shifting 
macroeconomic landscape. 
2. Introduction 
a. Background and Context 
Egypt’s financial market has experienced significant macroeconomic shifts over the past decade, 
including currency devaluation, high inflation, and aggressive monetary policy responses. Investors 
navigating this environment require robust, data-driven tools to evaluate risk and optimize portfolio 
allocation. This project focuses on constructing an analytical framework for an Egyptian investment 
portfolio, leveraging historical price data across equities, commodities, fixed income, and real estate 
stocks. By applying modern data science techniques, we aim to transform raw market data into 
predictive insights and actionable investment signals. 
b. Literature Review 
The theoretical foundation of this project rests on Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT), pioneered by 
Markowitz (1952), which demonstrates that optimal asset allocation relies on maximizing 
expected return for a given level of risk through diversification and covariance analysis. In 
emerging markets like Egypt, MPT principles are critically tested by structural volatility, 
inflationary pressures, and currency depreciation. Empirical reports from the International 
Monetary Fund (IMF) and the Central Bank of Egypt (CBE) consistently highlight how EGP 
devaluation amplifies equity risk while simultaneously elevating the attractiveness of hard-asset

hedges such as gold and short-term sovereign debt (IMF , 2023; CBE, 2024). These 
macroeconomic dynamics necessitate portfolio frameworks that explicitly account for cross-
asset correlations and regime shifts. 
Recent quantitative finance literature emphasizes the limitations of traditional linear 
econometric models in capturing non-linear market dynamics and fat-tailed return distributions. 
Consequently, machine learning techniques—including ensemble methods (e.g., Random 
Forest, XGBoost) and kernel-based approaches (e.g., SVM, SVR)—have gained traction for regime 
classification and return forecasting, frequently outperforming conventional benchmarks in 
volatile environments (Gu, Kelly, & Xiu, 2020). However, few studies apply these methodologies to 
a comprehensive, multi-asset Egyptian portfolio framework that integrates real estate equities, 
commodities, and fixed income. This project bridges that gap by combining rigorous data 
engineering, advanced ML modeling, and interactive visualization to deliver empirically grounded 
portfolio strategies tailored to Egypt’s unique macroeconomic conditions. 
c. Project Objectives 
• Build a reproducible data science pipeline for multi-asset financial data 
• Perform rigorous EDA to understand risk profiles across asset classes 
• Engineer predictive features for ML modeling 
• Extract actionable, data-driven insights for portfolio allocation

c. Models Chosen 
After several trials and testing processes, we found that those models gave the optimal portfolio in the 
Egyptian market. 
i. Classification models  
1. Logistic Regression 
Purpose: Serves as an interpretable baseline classifier that estimates market regime probabilities using 
a SoftMax function. 

--- PAGE 15 ---
   
 
12 | P a g e  
 
Configuration: Trained on the reduced “lr” feature set using class_weight="balanced" with a maximum of 
1,000 iterations. 
Performance: Achieved accuracy of 56.9% on both EGX30 and Gold. The weighted F1-score reached 
56.7% for EGX30 and 52.6% for Gold, with an average weighted F1-score of approximately 54.6%. 
 
2. SVM (Support Vector Machines) 
Purpose: Captures non-linear market regime boundaries by projecting features into a higher-
dimensional space. 
Configuration: Implemented using an RBF kernel with C=1.2, class_weight="balanced" , and 
probability=True for calibrated probability outputs. 
Output: SVM was preferred over Decision Trees during final selection because Decision Trees achieved 
only approximately 48.1% mean accuracy across assets, compared to stronger and more stable 
performance from Logistic Regression and XGBoost. 
3. XGBoost 
Purpose: Acts as the primary high-capacity classifier by sequentially correcting prediction errors through 
gradient-boosted decision trees. 
Configuration: Trained with n_estimators=200, learning_rate=0.05, max_depth=5, subsample=0.8, and 
colsample_bytree=0.8. 
Performance: Achieved the highest classification performance among the tested classifiers, with 
accuracies of 60.7% for EGX30 and 55.9% for Gold. The weighted F1-scores reached 54.0% and 47.5%, 
respectively, resulting in an average accuracy of approximately 58.3%. 
  
i. Regression models 
4. Linear Regression 
Purpose: Provides a simple and interpretable baseline by minimizing residual sum of squares. 
Configuration: Trained on the reduced “lr” feature set with standard scaling applied before training. 
Performance: Produced the strongest regression results among all tested models. The model achieved 
an 𝑅2score of 0.920 on EGX30 and 0.940 on Gold, with a mean 𝑅2of approximately 0.930. RMSE values 
remained low at 0.00418 and 0.00573, respectively. 
5. Random Forest 
Purpose: Captures complex non-linear relationships using an ensemble of decision trees.

Configuration: Trained using n_estimators=200, max_depth=8, and min_samples_leaf=5 on the full 
feature set. 
Performance: Achieved an 𝑅2score of 0.841 for EGX30 and 0.535 for Gold, with a mean 𝑅2of 
approximately 0.688. The model showed stronger performance on EGX30 while maintaining reasonable 
generalization capability. 
6. SVR (Support Vector Regressor) 
Purpose: Models smooth non-linear return relationships using an epsilon-insensitive regression 
approach. 
Configuration: Implemented with an RBF kernel using C=1.0, epsilon=0.01, and automatic gamma 
scaling. 
Output: Achieved an 𝑅2score of 0.634 on EGX30 and 0.099 on Gold, with an average 𝑅2of approximately 
0.367. Although it produced the weakest standalone performance, the model remained useful for 
capturing alternative non-linear patterns and improving disagreement-based confidence estimation 
within the ensemble framework.

Results 
EDA Key findings 
The exploratory analysis revealed noticeable differences in liquidity, volatility, and return behavior across 
assets. EGX30 and Gold showed higher volatility and stronger price movement compared to more stable 
assets. Correlation analysis also indicated varying relationships between asset classes, supporting the 
use of diversification in portfolio construction. 
Additionally, some assets exhibited periods of stale or limited movement, reflecting lower market activity 
compared to more liquid instruments. 
Regression Results (Strategic Forecasting) 
The regression models were evaluated using 𝑅2, RMSE, and MAE metrics through walk-forward 
validation. 
Linear Regression achieved the strongest overall performance, with an average 𝑅2score of approximately 
0.93 across EGX30 and Gold, indicating that the engineered features captured a strong linear relationship 
with future returns.

Random Forest achieved moderate predictive performance with a mean 𝑅2of approximately 0.69, while 
SVR produced the lowest average 𝑅2score of approximately 0.37. 
These results suggest that simpler linear relationships explained a significant portion of market behavior, 
while non-linear models captured additional but less consistent patterns. 
Classification Results (Tactical Signals) 
Classification models were evaluated using Accuracy, Precision, Recall, and weighted F1-score metrics. 
The SVM classifier achieved the strongest tactical classification performance with approximately 37.8% 
accuracy and 37.5% weighted F1-score, slightly outperforming Logistic Regression, which achieved 
approximately 34.9% accuracy and weighted F1-score. 
Although classification accuracy remained close to the theoretical baseline of a three-class problem, the 
models still provided useful probabilistic signals and confidence estimates for tactical portfolio 
adjustments.

Conclusion 
This study demonstrated how machine learning and data-driven portfolio analysis can support 
investment decision-making in the Egyptian market. Exploratory analysis highlighted differences in 
volatility, liquidity, and correlation across assets, emphasizing the value of diversification through assets 
such as Gold and T-Bills. Among the tested models, Linear Regression achieved the strongest regression 
performance, while XGBoost and SVM provided the most effective classification results for tactical 
market signals. Walk-forward backtesting further showed that combining predictive models with portfolio 
optimization improved risk-adjusted portfolio stability across different market conditions. Despite 
challenges related to market volatility, currency effects, and policy-driven regime shifts, the proposed 
framework provides a practical foundation for data-driven portfolio management and future expansion 
using real-time data and more advanced forecasting models.
